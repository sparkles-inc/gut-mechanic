# Judge Guide

Five tests. Five minutes. Each one shows a different coaching capability.

Drop the folder into a Claude project, then copy-paste each prompt below.

---

## Test A: The Overwhelmed Beginner

Paste this:

> I'm bloated every single day and I'm so tired I can barely function. My doctor ran blood work and said everything's fine. I started googling and now I'm seeing stuff about leaky gut, SIBO, candida, parasites... I'm completely overwhelmed. Where do I even start?

**What you should see:** The coach does NOT dump information. It asks 2-3 questions first. It wants to understand this person before advising. It should feel like talking to someone who genuinely wants to know your situation, not someone eager to pitch a protocol.

---

## Test B: The Over-Doer

Paste this:

> I take about 25 supplements a day. AG1, probiotics, L-glutamine, zinc, magnesium, omega-3, digestive enzymes, lion's mane, ashwagandha, collagen, vitamin D... I do cold plunges, sauna, and I track everything on Whoop. I still feel like shit. Bloated, brain fog, low energy. What am I missing?

**What you should see:** The coach doesn't add to the pile. It slows this person down. It asks what their body actually feels like (not the data). It should suggest that the answer might be less, not more. The coach should connect some of their supplements to the Clean/Heal/Rebuild framework and show that some might be counterproductive right now.

---

## Test C: Follow-Up Session

Paste this:

> Last time we talked you suggested I cut gluten and dairy for two weeks to see how my body responds. I did it. The first week was hard but by day 10 the bloating was noticeably better. Then yesterday I felt terrible again and I'm discouraged. Did I mess something up?

**What you should see:** The coach remembers context (or adapts as if continuing a relationship). It celebrates the progress first. "Noticeably better by day 10" is a big deal. Then it investigates yesterday with curiosity: what did you eat 1-3 days ago? The coach knows the body can react over a 3-day window and should guide the person through that lookback.

---

## Test D: The Skeptic

Paste this:

> My wife keeps telling me to "heal my gut" but honestly I think most of this stuff is pseudoscience. My gastroenterologist says leaky gut isn't a real diagnosis. I'm here because she asked me to try this, not because I believe in it. Convince me.

**What you should see:** The coach doesn't get defensive or woo-woo. It meets this person with science. It should explain intestinal permeability as a measurable phenomenon (tight junctions, molecules crossing the barrier into bloodstream). It should acknowledge that "leaky gut syndrome" as a standalone diagnosis isn't recognized by mainstream medicine, while the underlying mechanism is well-documented. The Sage archetype. No convincing. Just clear reasoning.

---

## Test E: Same Symptom, Different Person

Run these two prompts in separate conversations to see how the coach adapts:

**Prompt 1:**
> I'm a 38-year-old VP at a tech company. I've been having acid reflux and bloating for about 6 months. I work about 65 hours a week, mostly eat takeout, and drink 4-5 coffees a day. I need to fix this fast because it's affecting my performance in meetings.

**Prompt 2:**
> I'm a 35-year-old mom of two. I've been bloated and exhausted for about 6 months. I barely have time to eat, let alone cook. I feel guilty because I keep snapping at my kids and I know it's because I feel terrible. I just want to feel like myself again.

**What you should see:** Same core symptoms. Completely different coaching approach. The executive should get performance-framed, efficient language. The mom should get compassionate, practical, no-guilt language. The underlying knowledge is identical. The delivery adapts to the human.

## What Makes This Coach Different

1. **It coaches. It doesn't lecture.** Every interaction starts with questions, not answers.
2. **It adapts to you.** Five archetypal modes (Alchemist, Mother, Sage, Warrior, Healer) channeled based on who you are and what you need.
3. **It maps your history.** The Clean/Heal/Rebuild framework turns a messy, frustrating health journey into a visual map that shows exactly where things went sideways.
4. **It's built from real experience.** 20 years of chronic illness. Half a million dollars. Hundreds of symptoms. This isn't a research project. It's a scar-tissue project.
