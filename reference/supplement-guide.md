# Supplement Guide Reference

## Core Principle

Supplements support. They don't replace diet and protocol work. You cannot supplement your way out of a bad diet, a toxic environment, or a gut that hasn't been cleaned. Supplements are the secondary layer, not the foundation.

Most supplements are garbage. This is not an exaggeration. The supplement industry is largely unregulated. Products are full of fillers, use poorly absorbed forms of nutrients, and make claims they can't back up. Cheap supplements are almost always bad. Expensive supplements aren't always good, but very cheap ones are reliably poor quality. Research matters. Form matters. Brand matters.

A $6.8 trillion wellness industry is trying to sell you the cure through one supplement. There is no silver bullet. There's a thousand golden BBs, taken in the right order, at the right time, while doing the foundational work that actually drives healing.

Binders are detailed extensively in detox-protocols.md. Quick reference here:

- **Zeolite:** Heavy metal binding, cage-like structure traps toxins
- **Activated charcoal:** Broad-spectrum toxin binding
- **Silica:** Aluminum and metal binding, gentle
- **Bentonite clay:** Volcanic clay, binds toxins, provides trace minerals
- **Psyllium husk:** Fiber-based, adds bulk, sweeps toxins out
- **Modified citrus pectin:** Binds heavy metals, can cross intestinal barrier

Take first thing AM on an empty stomach. Wait 60-75 minutes before food or other supplements. 4-6 mornings per week.

## Gut Healing Supplements

These support the repair of the gut lining directly:

### Colostrum
The first milk produced after birth, concentrated with immune factors and growth factors. Supports gut lining repair, immune function, and microbial balance. Bovine colostrum supplements are most common. Look for minimally processed, from grass-fed sources.

### Slippery Elm
A mucilaginous herb that coats and soothes the gut lining. Creates a protective layer over inflamed tissue. Gentle enough for most people. Can be taken as powder mixed in water, or in capsule form.

### Aloe Vera
Anti-inflammatory, soothes the gut lining, supports tissue repair. Use inner leaf gel (the latex/outer leaf can be a harsh laxative). Aloe vera juice or gel capsules. Good for people with inflammation and irritation in the digestive tract.

### Licorice Root (DGL)
Soothes and protects the gut lining. DGL (deglycyrrhizinated licorice) is the form to use, as it removes the compound that can raise blood pressure. Supports mucus production in the gut, which protects the lining.

### L-Glutamine
The primary fuel source for the cells lining the small intestine. Supports mucosal repair and maintenance of the gut barrier. Typically taken as a powder, 5-10g daily. One of the most well-researched supplements for gut repair.

### Acacia Fiber
A gentle prebiotic fiber that feeds beneficial gut bacteria. Much gentler than other fibers. Can be introduced earlier than most plant fibers for people working on rebuilding their microbiome.

## Nervous System Support

The nervous system determines whether the body can heal. These supplements support neurological repair and calming:

### Nervonic Acid
A rare omega-9 fatty acid critical for myelin sheath repair. The myelin sheath insulates nerve fibers and enables proper signal transmission. Damage to myelin is linked to neurological symptoms, brain fog, and nerve pain. Nervonic acid is found in certain seed oils (like lunaria oil). Supplementation supports the physical repair of nerve insulation.

### Lion's Mane Mushroom
Neuroprotective. Stimulates nerve growth factor (NGF) production. Supports myelin regeneration. One of the few substances known to actively promote nerve cell growth and repair. Take as extract (dual extraction, hot water and alcohol, captures both water-soluble and fat-soluble compounds).

### Gotu Kola
Stimulates brain tissue growth and regeneration. Supports memory, cognitive function, and connective tissue repair. Has been used in traditional medicine for thousands of years for brain and nervous system support.

### Minerals (See Minerals Section Below)
Minerals are the foundation of nervous system function. Without adequate minerals, no amount of nervous system supplements will work. Magnesium, zinc, selenium, and potassium are all essential for neurological stability.

## Anti-Anxiety Support

For people experiencing severe anxiety during the healing process. The anxiety often comes from histamine overload, mineral depletion, and a nervous system that's been in fight-or-flight for years.

### GABA Support
GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) is the brain's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter. It calms neural activity. When GABA levels are low, anxiety increases. GABA supplements can help, though absorption through the blood-brain barrier is debated. PharmaGABA (naturally fermented form) may be better absorbed.

### L-Theanine
An amino acid found in tea leaves. Promotes calm alertness without drowsiness. Increases alpha brain wave activity. Pairs well with GABA. One of the gentlest calming supplements available. 100-200mg as needed.

### Lithium Orotate (Low Dose)
At very low doses (5-20mg of lithium orotate, NOT the high-dose lithium used in psychiatry), lithium supports mood stability, neuroprotection, and brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) production. This is trace mineral lithium, not prescription lithium. Gentler and far lower dose. Can be helpful for anxiety, mood swings, and emotional reactivity during healing.

### CBD (Cannabidiol)
Anti-anxiety, anti-inflammatory, supports the endocannabinoid system which regulates mood, pain, and immune response. Quality matters enormously. Look for full-spectrum, third-party tested, from organic hemp. Can be helpful for acute anxiety episodes and for calming the nervous system enough to sleep.

## Digestive Support

### Digestive Enzymes
For people who can't break down food properly. Symptoms: bloating after meals, undigested food in stool, feeling heavy after eating. Digestive enzymes provide what the body can't currently produce enough of: proteases (protein), lipases (fat), amylases (carbohydrates).

Use for up to 1 year, then taper. The goal is for the body's own enzyme production to recover as the gut heals. Indefinite enzyme use creates dependency.

### HCl and Pepsin
For people with low stomach acid (which is far more common than high stomach acid, despite what acid reflux might suggest). 1-2 capsules at the beginning of meals. Helps break down protein and triggers the cascade of digestive processes that depend on adequate stomach acid.

## Minerals

Minerals are non-negotiable. The body needs them for every function. Enzyme reactions, nerve transmission, muscle contraction, bone formation, immune function. Without adequate minerals, nothing else works properly. Any restrictive diet (carnivore, elimination, GAPS Intro) without mineral supplementation will leave you depleted.

### Salt
Use good quality unrefined salt (Redmond's, Celtic, Himalayan). Not table salt. Real salt contains dozens of trace minerals in addition to sodium chloride. Liberally salt your food. People on restrictive diets often don't get enough sodium, leading to fatigue, headaches, and dizziness.

### Potassium
Essential for heart function, muscle contraction, and nerve transmission. Symptoms of deficiency: muscle cramps, weakness, heart palpitations, fatigue. Especially important on low-carb and carnivore diets where potassium-rich plant foods are removed. Supplement or use potassium-rich bone broth.

### Magnesium
Involved in over 300 enzymatic reactions. Most people are deficient. Supports muscle relaxation, sleep, bowel function, anxiety reduction, and detox pathways.

**Transdermal is more effective than oral for many people.** Magnesium applied through the skin (Epsom salt baths, magnesium oil spray, magnesium lotion) bypasses the digestive system and avoids the bowel issues that oral magnesium can cause.

**Forms for oral use:**
- Magnesium glycinate: calming, good for sleep and anxiety
- Magnesium citrate: bowel support, mild laxative effect
- Magnesium oxide: strong laxative, use occasionally, not daily
- Amino acid chelate: well-absorbed, daily use

### Magnesium Warning

Too much oral magnesium can cause a violent bowel reaction. Cramping, explosive diarrhea, significant discomfort. If the bowels are full of plant fiber when this happens, the reaction is worse. The fiber acts like an abrasive being pushed through at high speed. Start with a low dose and increase gradually. If you have a bad reaction, switch to transdermal application, which avoids the gut entirely.

## Iodine

Removes bromides, chlorine, and fluoride from the body (halide displacement). Nourishes the thyroid, which is essential for metabolism, energy, and hormonal balance. Important for detoxification. Lugol's solution or nascent iodine are common forms. Start low and increase gradually, as iodine mobilizes stored halides and can cause detox reactions.

## Vitamin C

Antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, immune support. Supports collagen production (important for gut lining repair). Required for adrenal function.

**Liposomal form absorbs dramatically better** than standard vitamin C. Standard oral vitamin C reaches bowel tolerance quickly (causes diarrhea). Liposomal vitamin C is encapsulated in fat particles that deliver it directly into cells, bypassing bowel tolerance limits.

## B-Complex

B vitamins are commonly deficient in people with gut issues (the damaged gut can't absorb them properly). They're needed for:
- Energy production
- Detox pathways (methylation)
- Nervous system function
- Mood regulation
- Stress response

Look for activated/methylated forms (methylfolate instead of folic acid, methylcobalamin instead of cyanocobalamin). These are the forms the body can actually use without needing to convert them.

Vitamin B1 (thiamine) specifically supports the migrating motor complex, which is essential for keeping food moving through the digestive tract.

## Glutathione

The body's master antioxidant. Essential for detoxification. Every cell uses glutathione to neutralize free radicals, process toxins, and support immune function.

**Must be liposomal form.** Standard oral glutathione gets destroyed in the digestive system before it can be absorbed. Liposomal glutathione is encapsulated in fat particles that protect it through digestion and deliver it into cells intact. This is one of the few supplements where the delivery form makes the difference between it working and being completely useless.

## Probiotics

Probiotics are not universally helpful. This is one of the most misunderstood supplements.

**When probiotics help:** When the gut needs beneficial bacteria reintroduced. When dysbiosis has reduced the population of beneficial organisms. When the person can tolerate additional bacterial input.

**When probiotics hurt:** When SIBO (small intestinal bacterial overgrowth) is present. If there's already too much bacteria in the small intestine, adding more makes the problem worse. Bloating that won't resolve, worsening symptoms on probiotics. These are signs to stop.

**Soil-based organisms (SBOs)** may be better tolerated than lactobacillus-based probiotics for people who react to standard probiotics. SBOs are hardier, survive stomach acid better, and don't require refrigeration.

**Fermented foods are nature's probiotic.** Homemade yogurt, kefir, sauerkraut, and other fermented foods provide diverse strains in their natural food matrix. But they're high histamine, which is problematic for sensitized people. Introduction must be extremely gradual (see gaps-protocol.md for the full fermented foods introduction sequence).

**Starting dose:** Tiny. As little as 1/18th of a capsule if very sensitive. Increase over weeks to months. Some people take months to reach a full capsule dose without die-off reactions.

## Fulvic Acid / Shilajit

Fulvic acid is a compound formed by the decomposition of organic matter over millions of years. It:
- Enhances mineral absorption (makes minerals more bioavailable)
- Supports detoxification
- Supports immune function
- Provides trace minerals

Shilajit is a natural substance found in mountainous regions that is rich in fulvic acid. It's been used in Ayurvedic medicine for thousands of years. Look for purified forms tested for heavy metals.

## Cod Liver Oil

A traditional GAPS supplement. Provides vitamins A and D in their natural forms, along with omega-3 fatty acids. Quality matters. Fermented cod liver oil is considered the highest quality but is controversial. Standard cold-processed cod liver oil is a reliable option.

## Warnings

### For Severely Anxious People

Many supplements may not be tolerated initially when the nervous system is highly activated. The body is in threat-detection mode. New inputs, even beneficial ones, can trigger reactions. Start with binders and basic gut healing supplements only. Add others as stabilization improves. Trying to take 20 supplements from day one is counterproductive. Less is more when the body is overwhelmed.

### Supplement Interactions

Supplements can interact with medications. Blood thinners, thyroid medication, psychiatric medications, and many others have interactions with common supplements. Always inform your doctor what you're taking. This isn't about getting permission. It's about safety.

### Quality

Most supplements are garbage. Fillers, wrong forms, low bioavailability, contamination. Research the brand. Look for third-party testing. Read the ingredient list (especially the "other ingredients" section where fillers hide). Price doesn't always indicate quality, but very cheap supplements are almost always bad. A supplement that costs $8 for 180 capsules is almost certainly not doing what the label claims.

## Timing Guide

Timing matters. Some supplements compete for absorption. Some need food. Some need an empty stomach.

- **Binders:** First thing AM, empty stomach. Wait 60-75 minutes before anything else
- **Digestive enzymes / HCl:** With meals (at the beginning of the meal)
- **Probiotics:** With or just before food (the food buffers stomach acid)
- **Minerals:** With food for best absorption (except magnesium, which can be taken anytime, including before bed for sleep support)
- **B-complex:** With breakfast or lunch (can be stimulating, avoid taking at night)
- **Fat-soluble supplements (A, D, K, E, liposomal forms):** With a meal containing fat
- **Glutathione:** On an empty stomach or with a small amount of fat
- **Calming supplements (GABA, L-theanine, magnesium glycinate):** Evening or as needed for acute anxiety

Space out supplements that compete for absorption. Iron and calcium shouldn't be taken together. Zinc and copper compete. When in doubt, split supplements between different meals.
