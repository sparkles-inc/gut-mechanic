# Food Guide Reference

## Overview

Food is medicine. It's also the most common source of ongoing damage for people with gut issues. What you eat, when you eat, and how you eat all matter. The body reacts to food on a spectrum from deeply nourishing to actively harmful, and where a specific food falls on that spectrum depends entirely on where YOU are in your healing.

Something that's "healthy" for a normal person can be devastating for a compromised one. Bone broth, fermented foods, nuts, seeds. All "health foods." All potentially triggering for sensitive people. Context is everything.

---

## Trigger Foods to Remove During the Clean Phase

These are the first things to go. They're the most common drivers of inflammation, histamine response, and gut damage:

- **Processed food:** Anything with ingredients you can't pronounce. Preservatives, colorants, emulsifiers, and industrial oils damage the gut lining and feed pathogenic bacteria
- **Sugar:** Even natural sugars when very sick. Sugar feeds dysbiosis. It feeds candida. It feeds pathogenic bacteria. The gut ecosystem cannot rebalance while being constantly fed sugar. All sugar gets cut initially. Even honey, even fruit. Reintroduce slowly during the Rebuild phase
- **Caffeine:** Destroys adrenal function. Stimulates cortisol. Keeps the nervous system in fight-or-flight mode. Must go during Clean
- **Alcohol:** Toxic to the gut lining, liver burden, inflammatory
- **Gluten:** One of the most reactive proteins for compromised guts. Directly damages the tight junctions in the intestinal lining
- **Dairy (initially):** Commercial pasteurized dairy is inflammatory for most people. Raw, fermented dairy may be reintroduced later during Heal phase through a careful introduction protocol

## Building Foods vs Cleaning Foods

This framework comes from the GAPS protocol and it's one of the most useful ways to think about food during healing.

**Building foods** are animal-based: meat, fish, eggs, animal fats, organ meats, bone marrow, fermented dairy. They build. They strengthen. They nourish. They provide the raw materials the body needs for tissue repair, immune function, and energy production. These are the foundation foods.

**Cleaning foods** are plant-based: vegetables, fruits, herbs, fiber. They detoxify. They sweep. They provide enzymes and phytonutrients that support the body's cleaning processes. These are important but secondary to building, especially when very sick.

### When to Eat What

**Too sick = building foods first.** A body that has no strength cannot handle the burden of detoxification. Cleaning requires energy. If the body is depleted, exhausted, and reactive to everything, it needs building before it can clean. This is why severely compromised people often do best on an all-animal or mostly-animal diet initially.

**As healing progresses, introduce more cleaning foods.** Once the body has strength and the gut is more stable, plant foods can be gradually reintroduced. They help the detox process. They add microbiome diversity. But they must wait until the body can handle them.

**The mistake most people make:** They start with a "healthy" diet full of plants. Salads, smoothies, raw vegetables. For a healthy person, great. For a compromised person, these are the hardest foods to digest and they overwhelm an already struggling system. They mean well. Their body can't handle it.

## Histamine Considerations

Histamine is a compound the body produces as part of immune response and healing. It's not inherently bad. But too much histamine in a sensitized person causes:

- Anxiety and panic attacks
- Weakened digestion
- Skin flushing and hives
- Headaches
- Insomnia
- Heart palpitations
- Brain fog

Chronic anxiety and panic attacks are strong indicators that histamine levels need to come down. When the body is sensitized, histamine gets triggered by almost everything. The body gets accustomed to feeling threatened. It becomes a self-fulfilling cycle where sensitivity increases over time if never addressed.

### High Histamine Foods to Be Aware Of

- Fermented foods (sauerkraut, kimchi, yogurt, kefir, kombucha, aged cheese)
- Aged and cured meats (salami, bacon, deli meats)
- Leftover cooked meat (histamine increases the longer cooked meat sits)
- Bone broth (long-simmered, higher histamine than meat stock)
- Vinegar
- Alcohol
- Canned fish
- Dried fruit
- Certain vegetables (tomatoes, spinach, eggplant, avocado)
- Certain fruits (citrus, strawberries, bananas)

### Why This Matters for GAPS

This is critical for people starting the GAPS protocol. GAPS relies heavily on fermented foods and bone broth, both of which are high histamine. A histamine-sensitive person who dives into these "healing foods" can have severe anxiety, panic attacks, and worsened symptoms. They think GAPS isn't working. In reality, they need to delay fermented foods, use meat stock instead of bone broth, eat the freshest possible meat, and address the histamine issue first.

### The Lowest Histamine Diet

Fresh meat. Almost nobody on the planet is allergic to meat. Cooked and eaten immediately. Not reheated. Not left over. This is why carnivore diets can be so effective for severely sensitized people. Not because meat is magic, but because it has the lowest histamine load and the fewest potential triggers.

## The 3-Day Reaction Window

The body can react to something over a 3-day period. This is crucial to understand and is one of the most common reasons people can't figure out what's triggering them.

You eat something on Monday. You feel fine Monday and Tuesday. Wednesday you feel terrible. You look at what you ate Wednesday morning and blame that. But the trigger was Monday's meal. It took 2-3 days for the immune response to fully manifest.

Track with a lookback. When symptoms appear, review not just today's food but the last 3 days. This changes everything for people who've been struggling to identify their triggers.

## Antinutrients

Plants contain compounds that interfere with nutrient absorption and add to the liver's processing burden:

- **Lectins:** Proteins found in grains, legumes, nightshades (tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, potatoes). They can damage the gut lining and trigger immune responses
- **Oxalates:** Found in spinach, beets, Swiss chard, almonds, chocolate, sweet potatoes. They bind to calcium and can form crystals in the body (kidney stones, joint pain)
- **Phytates (phytic acid):** Found in grains, nuts, seeds, legumes. They bind to minerals (zinc, iron, calcium, magnesium) and prevent absorption

In a healthy person with a functioning gut and liver, these compounds get filtered and processed without issue. In a recovering person, they overwhelm. Think of the liver as a bucket full of water. Every new input is another drop. When the bucket is full, every drop spills over. This is how people become sensitized to more and more foods over time.

Plants are the hardest food for humans to digest. Between antinutrients, fiber, and the processing demands they place on the digestive system, they require more from the body than animal foods do. This doesn't mean plants are bad. It means they're demanding. And a sick body can't afford the demand.

## Food Sensitivities Are a Symptom, Not a Permanent Condition

This is one of the most important things to understand. Food sensitivities are not your identity. They're not permanent. They're a symptom of leaky gut.

Here's the mechanism: the tight junctions between intestinal cells loosen. This allows larger molecules, toxins, and microbes to pass through the intestinal barrier directly into the bloodstream. The immune system encounters these molecules and reacts. That reaction is what you experience as a food sensitivity. Bloating, headaches, skin reactions, joint pain, anxiety, brain fog.

Fix the leakiness and the sensitivities resolve over time. As the gut lining heals, the tight junctions tighten. Fewer molecules escape. The immune system calms down. Foods that once triggered you become safe again.

This takes time. Months to years depending on severity. But it does happen. People who were reactive to 40+ foods end up eating broadly again.

## Meat Stock vs Bone Broth

These are different things with different effects.

**Meat stock:** Made from raw meat on the bone, particularly joints and cartilage. Cooked for 2-3 hours. Rich in gelatin, glucosamine, collagen, and amino acids. Usually clear and delicious. The gentler option. This is where sensitive people start.

**Bone broth:** Made from bones (raw, cooked, or a mixture). Simmered for much longer (8-24 hours). Requires vinegar in the water to leach minerals from the bones. Different nutritional composition. More intense. Higher histamine due to the long cooking time.

For histamine-sensitive people, meat stock is significantly better tolerated. Start with stock. Move to broth later when the body is more stable.

## The "One Man's Meat" Principle

No rigid diet prescriptions work for everyone. What nourishes one person can trigger another. The GAPS protocol teaches four sensory guides to determine what YOUR body needs:

1. **Desire:** What does your body genuinely want right now? Not cravings from addiction. Genuine desire after weeks of clean eating.
2. **Smell:** Does the food smell appealing? The body often rejects what it doesn't need through smell.
3. **Taste:** Does it taste good as you eat it? Nourishing good, not sugar-rush good.
4. **Satisfaction:** Do you feel nourished and content afterward? Or heavy, foggy, anxious?

These signals get buried under years of processed food and sugar. As healing progresses, they return and become reliable guides. Learning to trust them is part of the healing process.

## Food Reintroduction Protocol

During the Rebuild phase, foods are reintroduced carefully:

- **One food at a time.** Only one new food per test period
- **3-5 days between new foods.** Give the body time to react (remember the 3-day window)
- **Monitor for reactions.** Energy, digestion, mood, skin, sleep, pain levels
- **If a reaction occurs, remove the food and wait at least 30 days before trying again.** The gut is still healing. What triggers you now may be fine later
- **Keep a food diary.** Track what you introduce and how you respond

The goal is to expand the diet as broadly as possible over time. Every new food that's tolerated represents progress.

## Carnivore / Lion's Diet Context

For severely sensitive people, an all-meat diet (carnivore or lion's diet, which is typically just ruminant meat, salt, and water) can be the fastest path to stabilization. The reasoning is simple:

- Almost nobody is allergic to meat
- Meat has the lowest histamine load when fresh
- No antinutrients, no fiber, no plant compounds to process
- The liver gets a break from filtering plant compounds
- The gut gets a break from digesting fiber

This is not a permanent prescription for most people. It's a therapeutic intervention. The body needs building before it can clean. Carnivore provides building with minimal burden.

**Critical requirement:** Mineral supplementation is non-negotiable on carnivore. Salt (good quality), potassium, and magnesium. Without minerals, you will feel insanely depleted. Headaches, muscle cramps, fatigue, heart palpitations. The body needs minerals to function. Any restrictive diet without mineral supplementation will make you feel worse, not better.

## Sugar

Even "good" sugar (honey, fruit, maple syrup) feeds dysbiosis when the gut is compromised. Pathogenic bacteria and candida thrive on sugar. Every time you eat sugar, you're feeding the organisms that are making you sick.

Cut all sugar initially during the Clean phase. This includes fruit, honey, and all natural sweeteners. When the gut has healed enough (Rebuild phase), honey can be reintroduced first (it's the only sweetener allowed on GAPS). Then fruit, gradually.

For people with blood sugar instability, the GAPS protocol has a specific stabilization approach: mix raw butter (or coconut oil) with raw honey and eat small amounts frequently throughout the day while implementing the diet. This prevents the crashes that make sugar elimination feel impossible.

## Juicing

Juicing can be powerful for cleansing. The enzymes in fresh juice can help release mucoid plaque in the gut (a goopy layer of accumulated waste coating the intestinal lining). The concentrated nutrients are easily absorbed.

**But juicing has risks for sick people:**
- Can crash blood sugar, especially fruit juices. Adding fat (if tolerated) helps stabilize
- Very depleted people may not be able to handle the cleansing effect
- For very sick people, juicing should wait until they feel more grounded. Building foods first

**When juicing is appropriate:**
- After some stability has been achieved
- Using primarily vegetables with small amounts of fruit
- Good combinations: carrots, celery, apples, a little lemon, a dash of cabbage and beetroot
- For insulin resistance or diabetes: start with green juice only plus lemon, no fruit or sweet vegetables
- Best taken first thing in the morning, 20-30 minutes before breakfast

The GAPS milkshake version (juice blended with raw eggs and homemade sour cream) provides the cleansing benefits with added fat and protein to prevent blood sugar crashes.

## Eating Rules

How you eat matters as much as what you eat.

- **Chew thoroughly.** Digestion starts in the mouth. Salivary enzymes begin breaking down food before it reaches the stomach. Swallowing chunks bypasses this critical first step
- **Eat slowly.** Rushing meals keeps the nervous system in a sympathetic (stressed) state. Digestion requires parasympathetic (relaxed) state
- **Relax for 10 minutes after meals.** Give the body space to begin digesting without jumping into activity
- **No eating 2-3 hours before bed.** The body runs repair programs at night. A full stomach diverts resources from repair to digestion
- **Don't snack between meals.** Snacking turns off the migrating motor complex, the gut's self-cleaning mechanism. Leave 4-5 hours between meals for the MMC to do its sweep
- **Eat in a calm state.** Eating while stressed, anxious, or distracted impairs digestion dramatically. The body can't digest properly in fight-or-flight mode. Even the best food is poorly digested if you eat it in a state of stress
- **No starch and protein together.** They require different digestive environments
- **No fruit after meals.** Fruit ferments quickly and disrupts digestion of other foods eaten at the same meal
