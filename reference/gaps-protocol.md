# GAPS Protocol Reference

## What GAPS Is

GAPS stands for Gut and Psychology Syndrome / Gut and Physiology Syndrome. It was developed by Dr. Natasha Campbell-McBride, a neurologist and nutritionist who discovered the connection between gut health and brain function through her work with her own autistic son.

The core premise of GAPS: most chronic health conditions, both mental and physical, begin in the gut. "All diseases begin in the gut," as Hippocrates said thousands of years ago. GAPS builds on that truth with modern understanding of the microbiome, gut permeability, and the gut-brain axis.

The digestive system is the root system of the body. Before attending to the leaves and branches of symptoms, you attend to the roots first.

## Why GAPS Works

The root cause of most chronic conditions is gut dysbiosis: the microbial ecosystem in the gut has been disrupted. Adults carry roughly 2 kg of bacteria in the gut. There are more cells in that microbial mass than in the entire human body. When that ecosystem goes wrong, everything downstream goes wrong.

Gut dysbiosis leads to:
- Damage to the gut lining (leaky gut), allowing toxins and undigested molecules into the bloodstream
- Immune system activation and chronic inflammation
- Nutritional deficiencies (the damaged gut can't absorb properly)
- Neurological symptoms via the gut-brain axis (depression, anxiety, brain fog, panic attacks)
- Autoimmune conditions (the confused immune system attacks the body's own tissues)
- Skin conditions, hormonal disruption, chronic fatigue, pain syndromes

GAPS works because it addresses the root: repair the gut, restore the microbial ecosystem, and the downstream symptoms resolve.

### Conditions GAPS Addresses

**Gut and Psychology Syndrome:** Autism, ADHD/ADD, dyslexia, dyspraxia, addictions, depression, OCD, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, epilepsy, eating disorders. Brain function issues: mood swings, memory and cognitive problems, behavioral issues, panic attacks, anxiety, involuntary movements, tics, sensory processing problems.

**Gut and Physiology Syndrome:** Autoimmune diseases (celiac, rheumatoid arthritis, type 1 diabetes, multiple sclerosis, lupus, Hashimoto's), asthma, eczema, allergies, chronic fatigue syndrome, fibromyalgia, arthritis, endocrine disorders, digestive disorders (IBS, gastritis, colitis).

Mental and physical symptoms overlap significantly. People with mental conditions often have joint pain, fatigue, skin issues, and hormonal problems. People with physical conditions frequently develop depression, brain fog, and anxiety. The gut connects all of it.

### How Gut Flora Gets Damaged

- Bottle feeding instead of breastfeeding (babies acquire their initial gut flora from their mothers)
- Multiple courses of antibiotics (wipe out beneficial bacteria)
- Oral contraceptive use
- Processed food and fast food diets
- Chronic stress
- Environmental toxins

Mothers of children with GAPS conditions show gut dysbiosis in nearly 100% of cases. The damage passes generationally.

### Common Nutritional Deficiencies in GAPS Patients

Minerals: magnesium, zinc, selenium, copper, calcium, manganese, sulfur, phosphorus, iron, potassium, vanadium, boron. Vitamins: B1, B2, B3, B6, B12, C, A, D, folic acid, pantothenic acid. Other: omega-3/6/9 fatty acids, taurine, alpha-ketoglutaric acid, glutathione, various amino acids.

These deficiencies aren't from not eating enough of the right foods. They're from a damaged gut that can't absorb what it's being given.

## The Five GAPS Diet Approaches

GAPS is not one diet. It's five approaches tailored to different levels of sensitivity and different conditions. The severity of the person's situation determines which approach they start with.

### 1. GAPS Introduction Diet

**What it is:** A staged, progressive diet designed to rapidly heal and seal the gut lining. It does this through three mechanisms:
1. Supplies nourishing substances directly to the gut wall (amino acids, gelatin, glucosamine, collagen, fats, vitamins, minerals)
2. Removes irritating fiber and substances that damage sensitive gut tissue
3. Provides probiotic microbes in food form

**Who must do Intro:** Anyone with serious digestive symptoms. Chronic diarrhea, abdominal pain, bloating, reflux, blood or mucous in stool, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, acute or chronic gastritis, enterocolitis, esophagitis. Also anyone with food allergies or intolerances (these indicate leaky gut).

**Who can skip to Full GAPS:** People without serious digestive symptoms. People who are traveling or working and can't cook extensively. People with chronic constipation (Intro removes fiber, which constipation relies on, and may worsen it). People with compliance issues (start Full GAPS, do Intro later when ready).

**The stages:** The full stage-by-stage protocol is detailed in the GAPS book. The general progression:
- Stage 1: Meat and fish stock, boiled meats, well-cooked vegetables in stock
- Progressive stages add egg yolks, fermented foods (yogurt whey first, then yogurt), avocado, pancakes, and gradually more foods
- Final stages introduce raw foods, juicing, and baking with nut flours

**Duration:** Completely individual. Some people move through it in weeks. Others stay for months. Some complete it and return to Intro when something triggers a flare (infection, stress, accident).

**Getting stuck:** If you stall on a stage and can't progress, gut pathogens may need more than diet and probiotics alone. Explore natural anti-parasitic and anti-fungal remedies: oregano oil, olive leaf extract, grapefruit seed extract, herbal mixes.

**Important:** The GAPS Introduction Diet is NOT for pregnant or breastfeeding women. It initiates die-off reactions, toxins can pass into breast milk, and it can change milk composition. Pregnant and breastfeeding women should follow Full GAPS and include Intro healing foods (meat stock, soups, stews).

### 2. Full GAPS Diet

**What it is:** The standard GAPS diet for most people. Broader than Intro but still removes all processed foods, grains, starchy vegetables, sugar, and commercial dairy.

**Duration:** About 2 years on average. Some people with milder conditions start reintroducing non-allowed foods within a year. Others must follow it strictly for many years or permanently, depending on the severity of their condition.

**What you eat:** About 85% of daily intake comes from meats (including organ meats), fish, meat and fish stock, eggs, fermented dairy, and vegetables (some well-cooked, some fermented, some raw). Baking and fruit are limited to snacks between meals.

**Key rules:**
- All food prepared at home from fresh ingredients
- No processed foods of any kind
- Generous natural fats with every meal (animal fats, butter, ghee, coconut oil, cold-pressed olive oil)
- Cook with rendered animal fats (not olive oil, which is damaged by heat)
- Fermented foods introduced gradually
- Keep a symptom diary
- No rigid meat-to-vegetable ratios. Listen to the body. Cold weather: more meat, more hot stock. Hot weather: lighter meals, more salad.

**Coming off GAPS:** When health problems have been resolved and you've been well for 6+ months. Test with homemade sourdough pancakes first (white wheat flour fermented with homemade kefir or whey for 2-3 days, add eggs, salt, melted fat, make thin crepes). Eat 1-2 and observe digestion for 4-5 days. If no problems, gradually introduce sourdough bread. Proceed slowly from there.

### 3. No-Plant GAPS Diet

**What it is:** An exclusively animal-based diet. No plants at all. Not a leaf, not a speck of anything from the plant kingdom.

**Who it's for:** People with the most severely compromised digestive systems. People who tried the Introduction Diet and even Stage 1 was too much. Conditions that may require No-Plant GAPS include FPIES, pediatric type 1 diabetes, ulcerative colitis, Crohn's disease, severe mental illness, and rheumatoid arthritis.

Humans can live entirely on animal foods with excellent health. Plants appear to be not essential. GAPS patients have thrived on No-Plant for 4+ years.

This approach makes sense when you understand that plants are the hardest food for the body to digest. They contain antinutrients (lectins, oxalates, phytates) and fiber. In a healthy person, the liver filters antinutrients without issue. In a severely compromised person, every additional thing the liver has to process is one more drop in an already overflowing bucket.

### 4. GAPS Ketogenic Diet

**What it is:** A version of GAPS that shifts the body to fat-based energy production, generating ketones.

**Who it's for:** Cancer, epilepsy, Lyme disease, chronic fatigue syndrome, obesity, severe mental illness. For the vast majority of GAPS patients, the standard approaches are sufficient. The ketogenic version is specifically useful when these conditions are present, particularly cancer.

Physiological ketosis is perfectly normal and healthy. It's distinct from diabetic ketoacidosis, which is a dangerous emergency state.

### 5. More-Plant GAPS Diet

**What it is:** A version of GAPS with increased plant proportions for people whose bodies ask for more plant matter.

**Who it's for:** People who feel better with more plants. People whose bodies request more plant matter in certain seasons or periods. Some meals can be vegetarian, but this is NOT a vegetarian diet. Meats, fish, eggs, and fermented dairy must continue.

**Requirements:** No diarrhea or abdominal pain should be present. Adjust plant-to-animal ratios based on what your body signals.

## Meat Stock vs Bone Broth

This distinction matters. They're different things with different uses.

**Meat Stock:** Made from raw meat on the bone. Cooked for 2-3 hours (enough to thoroughly cook the meat). Uses joints and cartilage, which are rich in gelatin, glucosamine, and collagen. The result is usually clear and delicious with excellent amino acid content. Vinegar is not necessary. This is the gentler option and where sensitive people should start.

**Bone Broth:** Made from bones (raw, cooked, or a mixture). Requires vinegar in the water to leach minerals from the bones. Simmered for much longer. Has a different nutritional composition and taste. More intense than stock.

Both are beneficial on GAPS. For sensitive people, start with meat stock. It's less likely to trigger reactions.

**How to drink it:** 10-15 minutes before a meal rather than with the meal. This helps stimulate stomach acid production.

## Fermented Foods Introduction and Progression

Fermented foods are essential to the GAPS protocol. They provide probiotic microbes in food form. But for sensitive people, they must be introduced with extreme care because fermented foods are high in histamine. If someone is histamine-sensitive (chronic anxiety and panic attacks are strong indicators), fermented foods may need to be delayed or introduced even more slowly.

### Yogurt vs Kefir

**Yogurt** is made with lacto-bacteria. It's fairly mild with a milder die-off reaction. Start here.

**Kefir** contains both beneficial bacteria AND beneficial yeasts. It produces a more aggressive die-off reaction. Introduce after yogurt is well tolerated.

Both must be homemade and fermented for a minimum of 24 hours. Commercial versions don't meet GAPS standards.

### Introduction Sequence

If severely reactive, drip yogurt or kefir through cheesecloth and start with the whey only (the clear yellow liquid).

1. Sensitivity test first
2. Start with 1 teaspoon whey added to soup or stock daily
3. After 3-5 days at 1 teaspoon, increase to 2 teaspoons
4. Continue increasing until drinking 1/2 cup whey daily
5. Then add 1 teaspoon homemade yogurt daily, gradually increase
6. After yogurt is tolerated, introduce kefir (1 teaspoon daily, gradually increase)

Optimal daily amount is individual. Many people find 2 cups daily works well. Over-consumption can cause constipation. If constipation develops, switch to fermenting cream rather than milk.

### Sauerkraut

Homemade is always preferred over store-bought. Ready in 5-7 days for basic fermentation. Longer fermentation (6 weeks) may decrease histamine levels, which is important for histamine-sensitive people.

## Dairy Introduction Structure

**Raw (alive) milk** means straight from the animal, unpasteurized and unhomogenized. It's full of enzymes, active vitamins, amino acids, and proteins. GAPS patients cannot drink raw milk initially. All fermented dairy must be introduced gradually first. Then raw milk can be tried. Never use pasteurized milk, even after recovery.

**Best milk type:** No single answer. Some people do better on cow's milk, some on goat's. Use locally available organic unpasteurized and unprocessed milk. A2 milk is preferred (from natural breeds: Guernsey, Jersey, African, Asian, French cows, as well as camels and goats). Commercial breeds produce A1 milk, which has been linked with disease.

**For constipation:** Replace high-protein dairy (whey, yogurt, kefir) with high-fat dairy: ghee, butter, sour cream.

## The "One Man's Meat" Principle

This is one of the most important concepts in GAPS. There are no rigid dietary prescriptions that work for everyone. What heals one person may trigger another. Your body knows what it needs if you learn to listen to it.

Four sensory guides determine what YOUR body needs at any given time:

1. **Desire** - what does your body genuinely want? Not cravings driven by sugar addiction or habit. Real desire, the kind you feel after weeks of eating clean.
2. **Smell** - does the food smell appealing? If your body doesn't want something, it often won't smell right.
3. **Taste** - does it taste good as you eat it? Not "good" like candy. Good like nourishing.
4. **Satisfaction** - do you feel content and nourished after eating? Or bloated, heavy, anxious, foggy?

These signals get buried under years of processed food, sugar addiction, and ignoring the body's messages. As healing progresses, they come back online and become reliable guides.

## Building Foods vs Cleaning Foods

This framework from GAPS is essential for understanding what to eat and when.

**Building foods** are animal-based: meat, fish, eggs, animal fats, dairy. They build strength, nourish tissues, and provide the raw materials the body needs for repair.

**Cleaning foods** are plant-based: vegetables, fruits, herbs. They detoxify, provide fiber, and help the body clean.

**When the body is too sick, it cannot clean. It needs building first.** A body that has no strength cannot handle the burden of detoxification. This is why severely compromised people often do best on an all-animal diet initially. Build the body up. Get it strong enough to clean. Then gradually introduce cleaning foods.

Plants are the hardest food for humans to digest. They contain antinutrients (lectins, oxalates, phytates) and fiber that requires significant processing by the gut and liver. In a healthy person, antinutrients get filtered by the liver without issue. In a recovering person, they overwhelm. Think of the liver like a bucket full of water. Every new input is another drop. When the bucket is full, every drop spills over the edge.

This is how people become sensitized to everything. In the most severe cases, food allergies develop to almost every food, leaving only meat as a tolerated option. This isn't a permanent state. It's a sign of how overwhelmed the system is. As healing progresses, the bucket empties, and more foods become tolerable.

## Food Combining Rules

- No starch and protein together in the same meal (they require different digestive environments)
- No fruit after meals (fruit ferments quickly and disrupts digestion of other foods)
- Chew thoroughly (digestion begins in the mouth with salivary enzymes)

## Supplement Recommendations from a GAPS Perspective

Supplements are secondary to the diet. The diet is the foundation.

**Probiotics:** Start extremely small (as little as 1/18th of a capsule). Increase very slowly. Some people take months to reach 1 capsule per day. People with ME/CFS or fibromyalgia need extra-slow introduction. If SIBO is present, adding more bacteria can make things worse.

**Cod liver oil:** Source of vitamins A and D.

**Seed and nut oil blend:** Must be cold-pressed and refrigerated. Don't supplement flax oil alone (too high in omega-3). Take in a mixture with other oils like evening primrose or starflower.

**HCl and Pepsin:** 1-2 capsules at the beginning of meals. Supports stomach acid production for people who can't break down food properly.

**Magnesium:** Amino acid chelate form for daily supplementation. Magnesium oxide occasionally as a laxative. Too much oral magnesium can cause violent bowel reactions, especially if the bowels are full of plant fiber.

**For die-off support:** Charcoal tablets, clay, diatomaceous earth, chlorella (introduce gradually).

## Natural Anti-Parasitics in GAPS

Introduced in order, each starting at small doses:

1. **Garlic extract:** Start small, work up to 5-10g per day for adults, 500mg-1g for children
2. **Olive leaf extract:** Start small, work up to 1-2g twice daily for adults, 500mg twice daily for children
3. **Oregano oil:** Start with 1-5 drops per day, work up to 30-40 drops for adults, 5-10 drops for children
4. **Grapefruit seed extract:** Start with 1-2 drops per day, work up to 10 drops 2-3 times daily for adults, 4-5 drops for children

These are used when someone is stuck on a stage and can't progress, suggesting gut pathogens need more than diet and probiotics alone.

## Condition-Specific Notes

### Constipation
- Start with Full GAPS, not the Introduction Diet (Intro removes fiber that constipation relies on)
- Use high-fat dairy (sour cream, butter, ghee) instead of high-protein dairy
- More cooked vegetables and gelatinous meats, reduce muscle meats
- Increase animal fat with meals
- Juicing with high-magnesium produce (oranges, celery, apples, carrots, cabbage, beetroot, greens)
- Start the day with fresh orange or grapefruit juice
- Eat cooked beetroot regularly
- Magnesium supplement daily. Magnesium oxide occasionally as a laxative
- HCl and Pepsin at the start of meals

### Diabetes (Type 1 and Type 2)
- GAPS can help both types
- Type 1 is autoimmune, born in the gut. As the gut heals, insulin dose may slowly reduce
- Type 2 is caused by processed carbohydrates. GAPS removes them, enabling healing
- Limit carbohydrate consumption, don't go heavy on honey or desserts
- Plenty of animal fats to keep blood sugar stable
- For juicing: start with green juice only with lemon, avoiding fruit and sweet vegetables. After a few months, may add carrot

### Candida and Yeast
- Cannot completely eliminate candida. Can only control it
- GAPS diet builds immunity over time to keep it in check
- Natural remedies: oregano, olive leaf, grapefruit seed extract, garlic
- Alkalizing helps: bicarbonate of soda (brush teeth, baths, 1/2 teaspoon in water internally)
- Yeast is employed by the body to absorb heavy metals. Overgrowth is linked to toxicity. Kill the yeast without addressing the metals, and the body will grow more yeast.
- High-fat, high-protein diet is best for candida patients

### Histamine Intolerance
- The GAPS diet is appropriate
- Introduce fermented foods very slowly (they are high histamine)
- Reactions to fermented foods will diminish over time as the toxic load reduces and the gut heals
- Longer fermentation of vegetables (6 weeks) may decrease histamine levels
- Chronic anxiety and panic attacks are strong indicators of histamine overload

### Autoimmune Conditions
- "All autoimmunity is born in the gut"
- GAPS heals the gut and restructures immunity
- GAPS can resolve conditions like celiac disease and other inflammatory bowel conditions, not only manage them
- This is a bold claim but backed by years of clinical work by Dr. Campbell-McBride

## Detoxification Practices in GAPS

**Enemas:** Water enemas, coffee enemas, and probiotic enemas are all used. Coffee enemas support liver detoxification. Not recommended during Stage 1 of the Introduction Diet; introduce later.

**Detox baths:** Epsom salt, unrefined sea salt, seaweed powder, medicinal clay, apple cider vinegar, bicarbonate of soda. Alternate between different types. Best at bedtime.

**Sauna:** Start with a few minutes and gradually increase time and temperature. Weekly sauna is a traditional healing practice.

**Sunbathing:** An important part of GAPS. Cholesterol plus vitamin D plus sulfur creates powerful detoxifiers. Expose skin to sunlight regularly.

**Oil pulling:** Hold a mouthful of cold-pressed oil for 15 minutes, then spit it out. Good for mouth and dental health.

**Skin care:** General rule: if you cannot eat it, don't put it on your skin. Use olive oil or coconut oil as moisturizer. Avoid all commercial products with man-made chemicals.

## Foods: What's Always Allowed, Never Allowed, and Conditional

### Always Allowed on GAPS
- All meats including organ meats and gelatinous cuts
- Fish (prefer deep-water small fish: herring, sprats, anchovies; avoid large carnivorous fish: tuna, shark, swordfish due to mercury)
- Meat stock and bone broth
- Animal fats (rendered tallow, lard, butter, ghee)
- Eggs (yolks raw when possible)
- Fermented dairy (homemade yogurt, kefir, sour cream, whey)
- Mature cheeses (traditionally fermented, no post-fermentation additives)
- Vegetables (well-cooked initially, then fermented, then raw as tolerated)
- Cold-pressed olive oil (for cold use only, not cooking)
- Coconut oil (safe for cooking)
- Raw organic honey (the only allowed sweetener)
- Nuts (raw, soaked overnight; later stages only)
- Ripe fruit (later stages only)
- Herbs and herbal teas
- Fermented vegetables
- Apple cider vinegar
- Dry wine, mead, and distilled spirits (occasional, small amounts, much later)

### Never Allowed on GAPS
- All grains (wheat, rice, oats, corn, and all others) until coming off the diet
- Sugar and artificial sweeteners
- All processed foods
- Starchy vegetables (potatoes, sweet potatoes, yams)
- Beans and legumes (until very late, if at all)
- Commercial dairy (pasteurized, processed)
- Soy products
- Commercial condiments
- Canned foods
- Coffee (initially; may be reintroduced much later)

### Conditional / Later Introduction
- Cocoa: many tolerate on Full GAPS once digestive symptoms are gone. Pure organic cocoa powder only
- Butternut squash: use instead of sweet potatoes
- Legumes: hard to digest, try tiny sprouted amounts only when digestion is much better
- Seaweed and algae: introduce when Intro is completed and no digestive problems remain
- Flax and chia seeds: allowed on Full GAPS, but very fibrous. Don't consume until diarrhea has cleared
- Stevia: fresh from the plant or dried is allowed. Processed stevia is not

## Critical Warnings

- The GAPS Introduction Diet is NOT for pregnant or breastfeeding women (triggers die-off, toxins pass into breast milk)
- Die-off reactions can be serious in severe conditions and must be controlled through very slow introduction of new foods and supplements
- Vomiting during Introduction Diet may signal hypoglycemia, not just die-off. Give apple or orange juice immediately
- Do not remove sulfur-containing foods even if sulfate-reducing bacteria overgrowth is suspected. Sulfur is essential for rebuilding the gut, immunity, and liver function
- Food allergy testing is unreliable (too many false positives and negatives). Better to heal the gut wall
- Amalgam fillings should not be removed during pregnancy or breastfeeding
