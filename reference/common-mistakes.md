# Common Mistakes Reference

These are the mistakes people make over and over when trying to heal their gut. Understanding them saves months or years of spinning wheels.

---

## 1. Skipping Clean and Going Straight to Heal

The most common mistake. And the most consequential.

People learn their gut is the problem, so they start doing gut healing things. Probiotics. Bone broth. Fermented foods. L-glutamine. Collagen. All healing actions. All good in the right context.

But they're doing them on top of a body that hasn't been cleaned. Toxins are still circulating. The colon is backed up. The liver is overwhelmed. Detox pathways are closed.

It's like painting over mold. The wall looks better for a week. Then the mold comes back through. You haven't solved the problem. You've covered it.

Healing supplements and foods work when they're landing in a body that's ready to use them. If the body is still in toxic emergency mode, it can't absorb and utilize healing inputs properly. Clean first. Then heal. The order is the framework.

---

## 2. Doing Things in the Wrong Order

Chelating heavy metals before healing the gut. Killing parasites while still inflamed. Taking supplements before opening detox pathways. Starting with intense cleanses before gentle ones.

The order matters more than most people realize. Each step creates the conditions for the next step to work. Skip ahead and you either get no results or make things worse.

Chelation before gut healing: metals mobilize but can't exit efficiently. They redeposit.
Parasite protocols while inflamed: parasites feed on the inflammation, hold tighter, and you create more inflammation trying to kill them.
Supplements before pathways are open: the body can't process them, they become another burden.
Liver cleanse before colon is moving: the liver dumps its load, the colon can't clear it, toxins reabsorb.

Every protocol has prerequisites. The sequence exists for a reason.

---

## 3. Too Many Supplements

The wellness industry sells the idea that more supplements equals more healing. People end up taking 20, 30, sometimes 40 supplements a day. Their counter looks like a pharmacy.

More is not better. The body can only process so much at once. When overwhelmed, it can't use what you're giving it. Each supplement requires processing by the liver. Each one adds to the total burden. When the bucket is full, every new drop spills over.

Sometimes the answer is LESS input, not more. Especially when someone is very sensitive. Strip back to the essentials: binders, basic minerals, one or two gut healing supports. Let the body stabilize. Add more only when it can handle more.

The people taking 30 supplements and still feeling terrible aren't failing because they're missing supplement #31. They're failing because their body is drowning in inputs it can't process.

---

## 4. Ignoring the Nervous System

Doing every protocol correctly while stressed, anxious, and stuck in fight-or-flight mode. The diet is perfect. The supplements are dialed in. The cleanses are happening. But the nervous system is screaming.

The body cannot heal in sympathetic state. Repair processes are suppressed when the body believes it's in danger. Digestion shuts down. Immune function shifts from repair to defense. Nutrient absorption drops.

People don't realize their constant anxiety is actively preventing their healing. They think it's a separate problem. An emotional problem. Something to address later. But it's the same system. The nervous system determines whether the body can use what you're giving it.

Calming down isn't a nice-to-have. It's foundational. And the good news: the gentle cleaning work itself is what calms the nervous system. Less toxins equals less inflammation equals less alarm. They work together.

---

## 5. Forcing Healing

"No pain, no gain" applied to gut healing. Pushing through terrible reactions. Increasing doses when the body is struggling. Adding more protocols when the current ones are already overwhelming.

This is a western cultural instinct that does not apply here. If a protocol makes you feel terrible and keeps making you feel terrible, that's not a sign it's working. That's a sign it's too much.

Some discomfort is normal. Detox reactions happen. You might feel headachy, tired, or moody as toxins mobilize. But those reactions are temporary. They pass. And you feel better afterward. That's the test. Uncomfortable but then better = detox reaction, probably fine. Miserable and staying miserable = the body saying stop or slow down.

The body cannot be forced to heal. Only supported. The Chinese finger trap: pull harder and it grips tighter. Relax and it opens. Healing responds to patience and gentleness, not aggression.

---

## 6. Following One Guru or Protocol Exclusively

No single system has the complete answer. The GAPS protocol from Dr. Natasha Campbell-McBride is extraordinary for gut healing and dietary guidance. Detox methodology is essential for clearing the accumulated toxic load that makes healing possible. Nervous system understanding ties it all together.

Following GAPS alone means you might miss critical detox protocols. Following detox methodology alone means you might miss the dietary precision that GAPS provides. Following any single person's system exclusively means you're limited by their blind spots.

The best approach integrates the strengths of multiple frameworks into a coherent order of operations. That's what Clean, Heal, Rebuild does.

## 7. Confusing Detox Reactions with Triggers

This is one of the hardest things to learn and it takes time.

Sometimes you feel worse because something is working. Toxins are mobilizing. Die-off reactions from pathogenic organisms release their own toxins. The body is processing a backlog of waste. This is uncomfortable but productive.

Sometimes you feel worse because something is triggering you. You ate a food you're sensitive to. You took a supplement your body can't handle right now. You pushed too hard. This is uncomfortable and counterproductive.

**General rule for telling them apart:**
- Detox reactions are uncomfortable but leave you feeling better afterward. The discomfort is temporary (hours to a couple of days) and resolves into feeling better than before.
- Triggers make you feel bad and keep you feeling bad. There's no "feeling better after" moment. You just feel worse.

This distinction isn't always clean or obvious. It develops with experience and body awareness. Keeping a detailed journal helps enormously. Over time, you learn your body's patterns.

## 8. Not Keeping Bowels Moving

Taking binders, doing cleanses, supporting the liver. But not having regular, complete bowel movements. This means toxins are being mobilized and bound but not leaving the body. They're sitting in the colon, waiting to be reabsorbed.

Bowel motility is not optional. It's the exit route. Everything else is pointless if the exit is blocked.

The migrating motor complex sweeps the gut clean between meals. Snacking turns it off. Magnesium, B1, and castor oil support it. If bowels aren't moving daily, this is a top priority. Everything else depends on it.

---

## 9. Starting Probiotics with SIBO

Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth (SIBO) means there's already too much bacteria in the small intestine, where there shouldn't be much. The bacteria have migrated up from the colon or overgrown due to poor motility.

Taking probiotics when SIBO is present is adding bacteria to an already overgrown situation. It's like adding more guests to an overcrowded room. The bloating gets worse. The symptoms get worse. The person thinks probiotics don't work for them or that their gut is unfixable.

Probiotics aren't universally helpful. They're context-dependent. If someone has been taking probiotics for months with no improvement or worsening symptoms, SIBO should be considered. The answer may be to stop probiotics entirely and focus on motility and clearing the overgrowth first.

## 10. Eating "Healthy" Foods That Are Actually Triggering

Bone broth. Fermented foods. Nuts. Seeds. Avocado. Spinach. These are health foods by conventional standards. Every wellness influencer promotes them. Every gut health article recommends them.

For sensitive people, they can be devastating.

- **Bone broth** is high histamine (long cooking time). Meat stock (shorter cook) is gentler.
- **Fermented foods** are among the highest histamine foods. For a histamine-sensitive person, they trigger anxiety, panic, and worsened symptoms.
- **Nuts and seeds** are high in lectins, phytates, and are hard to digest. Many people can't tolerate them until healing is well advanced.
- **Avocado** is moderate histamine and contains compounds some people react to.
- **Spinach** is extremely high in oxalates, which can cause joint pain, kidney issues, and inflammation.

Just because something is healthy for most people doesn't mean it's healthy for YOU right now. What the body needs changes as healing progresses. Something intolerable today may be fine in six months. The key is listening to your body's actual response rather than following a generic "healthy food" list.

## 11. Giving Up Too Soon

Healing from years or decades of accumulated damage doesn't happen in weeks. The body needs time. Gut lining repair takes months. Microbiome restoration takes months to years. Nervous system recalibration takes months. Toxic load reduction is an ongoing process.

Most people quit right before the breakthrough because the timeline doesn't match their expectations. They expected to feel dramatically better in 4-6 weeks. When they don't, they decide the protocol doesn't work and move on to the next thing. Then the next thing. Then the next thing. Meanwhile, they never gave any single approach enough time to actually work.

Consistency over months is what creates results. Not perfection. Consistency. Doing the basic things (binders, clean diet, open pathways, minerals, clean water) day after day. The body responds to sustained input, not heroic one-time interventions.

---

## 12. Not Tracking Reactions Properly

The body can react to something over a 3-day period. If you only look at today's food for today's symptoms, you'll miss the actual trigger every time.

Monday's lunch may be what's causing Wednesday's anxiety. Tuesday's dinner may be what's causing Friday's bloating. The immune response doesn't always happen in real time. It cascades over days.

Track with a 3-day lookback. When symptoms appear, review not just today but the previous 3 days. What did you eat? What supplements did you take? What changed? This is tedious. It's also the difference between identifying triggers and guessing endlessly.

A simple food and symptom diary, even just notes on your phone, transforms the guessing game into actual data.

## 13. Believing Your Genes Determine Your Fate

"I was just born this way." "It runs in my family." "It's genetic." These beliefs keep people stuck more effectively than any physical condition.

Genes load the gun. Environment and diet pull the trigger. Your genes won't express diabetes if you aren't eating the diet that leads to that. Your genes won't express autoimmune conditions if your gut is healthy and your immune system is regulated. Genetic predisposition is not genetic destiny.

The health epidemic is proof that something has changed in our environment and diet, not in our genes. Genes haven't changed in a generation. What's changed is what we eat, what we're exposed to, and how little we support our body's natural cleaning mechanisms.

Believing you're a special case whose body just can't heal is the most reliable way to ensure it doesn't. Not because belief is magic. Because belief determines whether you persist long enough for the work to pay off.

---

## 14. Trusting That Supplements Are High Quality

Most people grab a supplement off the shelf based on the front label and assume it works. They don't check the form of the nutrient. They don't read the "other ingredients." They don't research the brand. They don't know that cyanocobalamin (cheap B12) is dramatically less useful than methylcobalamin (active B12). They don't know that most oral glutathione is destroyed in the stomach and never reaches the cells.

Fillers, binders (the bad kind), flow agents, artificial colors, soy, corn derivatives, and other additives make up a significant portion of many supplements. The actual active ingredient may be in a form the body can barely absorb.

Research matters. The form of the nutrient matters. Third-party testing matters. Very cheap supplements are almost always bad. Expensive isn't always good, but cheap is reliably poor. A supplement that doesn't work isn't just a waste of money. It's a false sense of security that the gap is being covered when it isn't.

## 15. Not Getting Enough Minerals

This is the silent underminer. Any restrictive diet without mineral supplementation will leave you depleted. Carnivore. Elimination. GAPS Introduction. Keto. All of them remove foods that normally provide certain minerals.

The body needs minerals for every function. Nerve transmission, muscle contraction, enzyme reactions, bone formation, immune function, energy production. Without adequate salt, potassium, and magnesium, the body falls apart regardless of what else you're doing.

Symptoms of mineral depletion: headaches, muscle cramps, fatigue, heart palpitations, dizziness, anxiety, weakness, brain fog. People often blame these symptoms on the diet itself ("carnivore doesn't work for me") when the real problem is they forgot to supplement minerals.

Salt (good quality unrefined), potassium, and magnesium are the non-negotiable trio. They must be supplemented on any restrictive diet. This is not optional. This is the difference between a restrictive diet that heals and one that depletes.
