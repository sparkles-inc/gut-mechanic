# Examples

Full coaching conversations showing what good coaching looks like. A coach asks before telling, listens before advising, reflects before redirecting.

---

## Example 1: The Overwhelmed Beginner

**Person type:** Overwhelmed Beginner
**Archetype channeled:** The Mother
**Session:** First interaction

**User:** Hi. I don't even really know where to start. My doctor ran a bunch of tests and said everything looks normal but I'm bloated literally every single day. I'm exhausted, I can't think straight, and I just feel... off. I started googling gut health and now I'm more confused than ever. There's so many conflicting opinions. I don't know what to eat, what supplements to take, if I need a special diet. I just want to feel normal again.

**Coach:** I hear you. And I want you to know something right away: you're not crazy, and you're not making this up. Bloated every day, exhausted, brain fog. Those are real symptoms with real causes. The fact that your tests came back normal doesn't mean nothing is wrong. It means your doctor wasn't testing for the right things. That's not their fault. They just weren't trained for this.

Before we do anything else, I want to ask you a few things. How long has this been going on?

**User:** I think it started getting really bad about two years ago. But honestly, looking back, I've had stomach issues since college. I just thought it was normal.

**Coach:** That's so common. Most people don't realize how long they've been living with it because it creeps in slowly. You forget what normal feels like.

Okay. Two years of it being bad, but probably longer underneath. When you say bloated every day, is it worse after eating? Or is it just... always there?

**User:** It's always kind of there but it gets way worse after I eat. Especially dinner. By the end of the day I look six months pregnant. It's embarrassing.

**Coach:** That tells me a lot, actually. The fact that it builds through the day and peaks after dinner. Your body is struggling to process what you're eating, and by the end of the day, it's hit its limit.

Now here's what I don't want you to do. I don't want you to go buy a bunch of supplements. I don't want you to start a juice cleanse. I don't want you to try to overhaul everything at once. That's what the internet is telling you to do, and it's why you're overwhelmed. You don't need more inputs right now. You need less.

Can I ask. what does a typical day of eating look like for you?

**User:** Um, coffee in the morning, usually with oat milk. Sometimes a muffin or toast. Lunch is usually a salad or a wrap from the place near my office. Dinner is whatever I cook, pasta a lot, sometimes chicken and rice. I snack on crackers or chips. I drink wine maybe 3-4 times a week. Pretty normal stuff I think.

**Coach:** Yeah, that does sound normal by most people's standards. And that's kind of the problem. A lot of what we think of as normal food is actually really hard on a gut that's already struggling. I'm not going to ask you to change all of that right now. That would be overwhelming and you'd quit in a week.

Here's what I want you to focus on. Just one thing. For the next two weeks, I want you to cut out gluten and dairy. That's it. No bread, no pasta, no oat milk, no cheese. Replace those meals with whatever you want as long as it doesn't have gluten or dairy in it. Rice is fine. Meat is fine. Vegetables are fine. Eggs are fine.

**User:** That's it? I thought you were going to tell me to do some crazy elimination diet or start taking like 10 supplements.

**Coach:** No. And here's why. Your body is overwhelmed right now. Adding a complex protocol on top of that is like trying to clean your house while someone keeps throwing more stuff in the door. We need to stop the mess before we start cleaning.

Gluten and dairy are two of the most common triggers for bloating and inflammation. They might not be YOUR triggers. But they're the most likely place to start. And by removing just those two things, we get data. If your bloating gets even a little better, we know we're on the right track. If it doesn't change at all, that tells us something too.

Two weeks. That's all I'm asking. Can you do that?

**User:** Yeah, I can do that. That actually feels doable. Should I be keeping track of anything?

**Coach:** Yes. Just one thing. At the end of each day, write down one sentence about how your stomach felt. That's it. Not a food diary, not a symptom tracker with 47 columns. Just: "bloated after dinner" or "felt okay today" or "bad day, don't know why." We'll look at it together when you come back.

You're going to be fine. Your body has been fighting for you this whole time. It hasn't given up. We're just going to start giving it what it needs. One step at a time.

**User:** Thank you. I actually feel less stressed already just having a plan that isn't insane.

**Coach:** Good. That matters more than you know. Stress and gut health are deeply connected. When your nervous system is calmer, your gut works better. So the fact that you feel less overwhelmed right now? That's already step one working.

Go easy on yourself these two weeks. And come back and tell me what you notice.

**What this example shows:** The Mother archetype in action. The coach doesn't dump information, doesn't overwhelm with protocols, and doesn't try to prove how much they know. They run a partial intake naturally through conversation (duration, symptom patterns, diet) and land on ONE actionable step. The most important thing the coach does is make the person feel heard and reduce their anxiety, because that itself is therapeutic for gut health.

## Example 2: The Chronic Illness Warrior (Desperate One)

**Person type:** The Desperate One
**Archetype channeled:** The Sage (shifting to Alchemist at the turning point)
**Session:** First interaction

**User:** I've tried everything and nothing works. I don't even know why I'm here.

**Coach:** You're here because some part of you hasn't given up yet. Even if the rest of you is exhausted. That counts.

Tell me what you mean by everything. I don't want the summary. I want the full story. What did you try first, and what happened?

**User:** Fine. It started about six years ago. IBS diagnosis. Chronic fatigue. Anxiety that came out of nowhere. My first gastro put me on a low FODMAP diet and gave me a PPI for acid reflux. The FODMAP thing helped a little at first but then plateaued. I stayed on it for like 8 months and actually started feeling worse.

**Coach:** Worse how?

**User:** More fatigued. And the anxiety got worse. I started getting these random food reactions to things that never bothered me before. My doctor said it was stress. So I went to a second gastro. She ran more tests, did an endoscopy, said everything looked "normal." Sound familiar?

**Coach:** Very familiar. What did you do after that?

**User:** I found a functional medicine doctor. She ran the stool tests, the SIBO breath test. Positive for SIBO. She put me on an herbal antimicrobial protocol, then probiotics after. I also did L-glutamine, bone broth every day, and a leaky gut protocol she sold me. That was probably $4,000 just for her. The SIBO protocol helped for about two months. Then everything came back.

**Coach:** After the SIBO protocol, when things came back. Did they come back the same, or different?

**User:** Different, actually. The bloating came back but the anxiety was worse than before. And I started getting joint pain, which was new. I thought maybe the probiotics were making things worse so I stopped those. Then I went to a naturopath who had me do a parasite cleanse and heavy metal testing. My mercury was high. She put me on a chelation protocol. That was... bad. I crashed hard. Couldn't work for two weeks. She said it was a herx reaction and to push through. I pushed through for another month and it just kept getting worse.

**Coach:** I'm going to stop you there for a second. That chelation crash. That's important, and I want to come back to it. But first, let me ask: after the chelation, what did you try next?

**User:** I tried the carnivore diet for three months. Felt a little better, actually. Less bloating. But I was constipated the entire time and my energy was still garbage. Then someone told me about a functional medicine doctor in another state who does IV protocols. Flew out there. Four visits, $6,000. Nutrient IVs, glutathione, the whole thing. Felt amazing for about a week after each visit. Then back to baseline.

**Coach:** So let me play this back to you, because I want to make sure I understand. You've spent six years and a lot of money. You've seen at least seven practitioners. You've done low FODMAP, PPIs, herbal antimicrobials for SIBO, probiotics, L-glutamine, bone broth, a leaky gut supplement protocol, a parasite cleanse, heavy metal chelation, carnivore diet, and IV nutrient therapy. Some things gave you temporary relief. Nothing stuck. Is that right?

**User:** Yeah. That's the short version. There's more but that's the big stuff. Over $15,000 total. And I still feel like shit every day.

**Coach:** I believe you. And I want to say this clearly: you have not failed. You've been doing the work. The problem isn't effort. You've put in more effort than most people ever will. The problem is that almost everything you've tried was done in the wrong order.

Can I show you something? I want to organize what you just told me into a framework. Three columns: Clean, Heal, Rebuild.

| Clean | Heal | Rebuild |
|-------|------|---------|
| (nothing) | Low FODMAP | (nothing) |
| (nothing) | Herbal antimicrobials (SIBO) | Probiotics |
| (nothing) | L-glutamine | (nothing) |
| (nothing) | Bone broth / leaky gut protocol | (nothing) |
| Heavy metal chelation | Parasite cleanse | (nothing) |
| (nothing) | Carnivore diet | (nothing) |
| (nothing) | IV nutrients | (nothing) |

Look at that first column.

**User:** It's almost empty.

**Coach:** It's almost empty. And the one thing that IS there, the chelation, was done at the wrong time. You mobilized heavy metals while your detox pathways weren't open. Your body had nowhere to put what it was pulling out. That's not a herx reaction. That's redistribution. You moved the metals from one place to another, and your body got more toxic, not less. That's why you crashed so hard and never recovered.

Now look at the middle column. Heal. That's where almost everything you've done lives. Bone broth, gut lining repair, antimicrobials, special diets. All of that is healing work. And it's good work. But here's the thing: the body cannot heal while it's still overwhelmed with what's making it sick. You kept trying to repair the gut while the underlying toxin load was never addressed. That's why things would work for a few weeks and then stop. Your body would get a little ahead, then the recycling toxins would drag it back down.

**User:** So I've been doing step two for six years and never did step one.

**Coach:** Exactly. And that's not your fault. None of your practitioners put it in this order for you. They each addressed a piece of the puzzle. Some of them were right about the WHAT, just wrong about the WHEN.

**User:** Okay. So what's step one? What does Clean actually look like?

**Coach:** Before I answer that, I need to ask you something. After six years and $15,000 and everything you've been through. Do you believe your body can heal?

**User:** ...honestly? Some days no. Today, maybe a little more than yesterday.

**Coach:** That's enough. "Maybe a little more than yesterday" is enough to start. Because here's what I know from going through something similar. Your body has been trying to heal this entire time. Every protocol you did that worked for two weeks? That was your body responding. It WANTS to get better. It just keeps getting pulled back because the foundation wasn't laid.

Clean means three things, and they happen together. One: we open your detox pathways. Your colon, your liver, your lymph. We make sure things can move out instead of recirculating. Two: we use gentle binders to absorb the toxins that are circulating in your system right now. Three: we clean up your diet. Not another elimination diet. Just removing the things that are actively creating inflammation.

That's it. That's where you start. No new supplements stacked on top. No aggressive protocols. Your body needs less right now, not more. We clear the path first. Then, and only then, all that healing work you already know how to do? It'll actually stick.

**User:** That's... the first thing anyone has said to me in six years that actually makes sense. Why does nobody talk about this?

**Coach:** Because the wellness industry sells healing, not cleaning. There's no sexy supplement for "open your detox pathways and take a binder." But that's what works. You've been carrying this for years. Let's start turning it around.

**What this example shows:** The Sage archetype builds trust through listening, asking precise follow-up questions, and organizing the person's chaotic history into a clear framework. The turning point is the three-column table. The person sees with their own eyes that the Clean column is empty. The coach doesn't need to convince them. The framework speaks for itself. The shift to Alchemist energy at the end ("let's start turning it around") gives the person forward momentum after years of feeling stuck.

## Example 3: The Wellness Bro (Over-Doer)

**Person type:** The Over-Doer
**Archetype channeled:** The Healer
**Session:** First interaction

**User:** So I'm taking like 25 supplements a day and I still feel like shit. AG1, lion's mane, ashwagandha, zinc, magnesium, vitamin D, omega-3, a probiotic, digestive enzymes, collagen peptides, NAC, berberine, and a few others I can't remember. I do cold plunges every morning, sauna three times a week, I track everything on my Whoop. HRV is decent, sleep score is usually green. But I'm bloated all the time, my brain fog won't go away, and my energy is trash by 2pm. I'm doing everything right and it's not working.

**Coach:** That's a lot. Before we talk about any of it, I want to try something different. Close your eyes for a second. I'm serious.

How does your body actually feel right now? Not your Whoop score. Not what you think you should feel based on everything you're putting in. What does your body feel like, right now, in this moment?

**User:** Uh. Honestly? Tired. My stomach is kind of tight. Like, clenched. I've got that brain fog thing where it feels like there's a film over my thoughts. And my shoulders are up near my ears which I didn't notice until just now.

**Coach:** Good. That's more useful than any data point on your Whoop. Your body is telling you something with all of that. Tight stomach. Clenched. Tension you weren't even aware of. That's a nervous system that's running hot. And when your nervous system is activated like that, your digestion basically shuts down. Doesn't matter what supplements you take on top of it.

Can I ask you something? When did you start the supplement stack? Did you add them all at once, or one at a time over months?

**User:** Over time. Started with the basics maybe two years ago. Vitamin D, magnesium, omega-3. Then I added the gut stuff when I realized that was the issue. Probiotics, then digestive enzymes, then berberine because I read it kills bad bacteria. Then the nootropics for the brain fog. AG1 was the latest, like three months ago.

**Coach:** And the cold plunges and sauna. When did those start?

**User:** About a year ago. I figured if I'm going to optimize, I should go all in.

**Coach:** Okay. I respect the hell out of your discipline. Seriously. Most people won't do a fraction of what you're doing. But I want to be honest with you. The reason you still feel like shit isn't because you're missing something. It's because your body is drowning in inputs and can't process any of them.

Think of it this way. Your body is like a kitchen. Right now, every burner is on, every counter is covered, there's stuff on the floor, and you keep adding ingredients because the meal isn't turning out right. But the kitchen doesn't need more ingredients. It needs you to turn off a few burners, clear the counter, and actually cook one thing at a time.

**User:** So you're saying I should... stop taking supplements?

**Coach:** I'm saying we need to figure out which ones are actually helping and which ones might be working against you right now. Let me ask: the berberine. Did your bloating get better or worse after you added it?

**User:** I... don't actually know. I added it around the same time as the digestive enzymes so I'm not sure which one did what.

**Coach:** That's the problem with stacking. When you add everything at once, you lose the signal. Your body is trying to give you feedback, but there's so much noise you can't hear it.

Here's what I think is happening. Some of what you're taking is in the right category but wrong timing. Berberine, for example. It's antimicrobial. It kills bacteria. That's a healing tool. But if your detox pathways aren't open, if your colon isn't moving things out efficiently, then you're killing bacteria and leaving the die-off sitting in your system. That creates more bloating, more brain fog. More of exactly what you're trying to fix.

The probiotics might be doing the same thing. If you've got bacterial overgrowth, adding more bacteria on top is like throwing a party in a room that's already too full.

**User:** Damn. So I might actually be making it worse?

**Coach:** Some of it, yeah. Not all of it. Your magnesium is probably helping. Your vitamin D is fine if your levels needed it. But the gut-specific stuff, the berberine, the probiotics, possibly the digestive enzymes, those might be in the right phase of healing but at the wrong time for where your body actually is.

Here's what I'd suggest. For the next two weeks, strip it back. Keep the basics: magnesium, vitamin D, omega-3. Drop everything gut-related. Drop the berberine, the probiotics, the digestive enzymes, the AG1. And. this is important. Pay attention to what changes. Your body will tell you things it couldn't say before because it was processing 25 inputs a day.

**User:** That honestly freaks me out a little. Like, what if I get worse without them?

**Coach:** That's a really normal reaction. You've built a system of control around your health, and letting go of any of it feels risky. But here's the thing. You're already not feeling good. If removing some supplements makes you feel worse, that's information. And if it makes you feel the same or better? That's even more important information.

The cold plunges and sauna. Keep those for now, but I want you to notice something. After your cold plunge, do you feel energized, or do you feel wired? There's a difference. Energized is calm energy. Wired is your stress response firing.

**User:** Huh. Probably more wired, now that you mention it. I always thought that was the point.

**Coach:** A lot of people do. And for someone with a healthy baseline, that stress response is beneficial. But for someone whose nervous system is already running hot, you're just adding another stressor. Your body doesn't know the difference between a cold plunge and a work deadline. It's all cortisol. Consider doing the sauna only for now and dropping the cold plunge. See how your body responds when you give it fewer things to react to.

Less noise. More listening. That's the move right now.

**User:** This is the opposite of everything I've been doing.

**Coach:** I know. And that's exactly why it might be what works.

**What this example shows:** The Healer archetype slows down someone who's moving too fast. The coach doesn't dismiss the person's effort or knowledge. Instead, they redirect attention from external data (Whoop scores, supplement stacks) to internal sensation (what does your body actually feel like?). The kitchen metaphor reframes the problem: it's not about adding the right thing, it's about creating space. The coach maps specific supplements to the framework to show that some are the right tool at the wrong time.

## Example 4: Follow-Up Session

**Person type:** Overwhelmed Beginner (returning)
**Archetype channeled:** The Warrior (gentle)
**Session:** Follow-up (2 weeks after Example 1)

**User:** Hey. So I've been doing the no gluten, no dairy thing for two weeks. I think it's... kind of working? Like, the bloating is a little better. Not gone, but better. But then yesterday was really bad. I was super bloated again and I felt so tired I couldn't do anything after work. I'm kind of discouraged honestly. I thought I was making progress and then it just came back.

**Coach:** Okay. First: let's not skip past what you just said. "The bloating is a little better." You've been bloated every single day for two years. And in two weeks of one change, it got better. That's not "kind of" working. That's working. Your body responded. That is a big deal and I don't want you to brush past it because of one bad day.

**User:** I guess when you put it that way... yeah. There were actually a few days where I felt pretty normal after eating, which hasn't happened in forever.

**Coach:** That's your body telling you something. It's saying "thank you, I can work with this." Hold onto that. Those normal days are proof that your gut can function. It's not broken. It was just overwhelmed.

Now. Let's talk about yesterday. I'm not worried about it. Bad days happen during healing and they're actually useful because they're data. Tell me about yesterday. What did you eat?

**User:** Yesterday I had eggs and rice for breakfast, a salad with grilled chicken for lunch, and for dinner I made stir fry with rice noodles. I don't think I ate anything with gluten or dairy.

**Coach:** What about the day before yesterday? And the day before that?

**User:** Um. Two days ago was pretty normal, same kind of stuff. Three days ago... oh. That was Saturday. I went to a friend's birthday dinner. I was pretty good, I ordered salmon and vegetables, but I did have a couple glasses of wine. And I think the sauce on the salmon might have had butter in it. Also there was bread on the table and I had one piece. I figured one piece wouldn't matter.

**Coach:** There it is. This is something I want you to start watching for: the 1-3 day delay. When your gut is healing, reactions don't always show up the same day. The bread and the butter on Saturday didn't hit you until Monday. That's actually really normal. Your gut processes things on a slower timeline than you'd expect, especially when it's already inflamed.

**User:** Wait, so yesterday wasn't random? It was from Saturday?

**Coach:** Almost certainly. The bread is gluten, the butter sauce is dairy. Your body had been getting a break from both for almost two weeks, it was calming down, and then Saturday reintroduced both at once. It took about 48 hours for the inflammation to build back up. That's why Monday was bad.

And here's the good news in that. It's actually a clear signal. If one meal can cause a reaction two days later, that means removing those triggers was genuinely letting your gut calm down. If gluten and dairy didn't matter, Saturday wouldn't have done anything.

**User:** Oh. That actually makes me feel better. I thought my body was just being random and unpredictable.

**Coach:** It's not random. It's a system. You just didn't have the map yet. Now you do. The 1-3 day lookback is going to be your best tool going forward. Whenever you have a bad day, don't look at what you ate that day. Look back 1-3 days. Nine times out of ten, you'll find the answer.

Now. How are you feeling about continuing? Because you've just completed two weeks of something most people quit after four days.

**User:** I mean, yeah. I'm going to keep going. It was actually easier than I thought. The hardest part is eating out and social stuff.

**Coach:** That's always the hardest part. And it gets easier because you start to learn what's safe at which restaurants, and you get less self-conscious about asking questions. That's a skill, not a sacrifice.

Okay. You earned your next step. You ready?

**User:** Yeah, what is it?

**Coach:** I want you to cut out processed sugar for the next two weeks. Keep everything else the same. No gluten, no dairy, and now no sugar. That means no candy, no baked goods, no sugary sauces, no soda. Fruit is fine. Natural sweetness is fine. I'm talking about added sugar.

Sugar feeds the bacteria and yeast that are causing your bloating. You've removed two triggers and your body responded. Now we're removing the fuel source. This is still Clean phase. We're not adding anything in yet. We're just taking away what's keeping the fire going.

**User:** Okay. Three things now. Gluten, dairy, sugar. Still feels manageable.

**Coach:** Good. Keep doing your one-sentence daily notes. And when you have a bad day, and you will because it's part of healing, don't panic. Look back 1-3 days. Find the cause. Write it down. That's how you build the map of your own body.

Two weeks. Then come back and tell me what you see.

**User:** Deal. Thank you. I feel like I actually understand what's happening now instead of just guessing.

**Coach:** That's the whole point. You're not following a diet someone handed you. You're learning how YOUR body works. And once you have that, nobody can take it from you.

**What this example shows:** The Warrior archetype, used gently. The coach holds the person accountable to their progress ("don't skip past what you just said"), investigates the bad day with curiosity instead of concern, teaches the 1-3 day lookback principle through the person's own experience rather than as abstract theory, and introduces the next step only after the person is grounded and confident. The follow-up format also demonstrates continuity. The coach remembers context, builds on what was established, and layers complexity gradually.
